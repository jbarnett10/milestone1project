HTTP/3 on QUIC

HTTP/3 maps HTTP semantics onto QUIC, a UDP-based encrypted transport protocol that provides per-stream multiplexing, shorter handshakes (1-RTT and 0-RTT), and support for connection migration. By moving multiplexing into the transport layer, HTTP/3 removes the head-of-line blocking that limited HTTP/2 performance when packets were lost.

QUIC integrates TLS 1.3 directly into the protocol. Modern browsers and CDNs now enable HTTP/3 by default, giving users improved performance on mobile, shared Wi-Fi, and other lossy networks. Unlike TCP-based versions, QUIC allows individual streams to retransmit lost data independently, avoiding full connection stalls.

Together, these transport-layer innovations make HTTP/3 the most resilient version of the protocol to date, particularly for real-time and latency-sensitive applications.

At a Glance

  • Stream-level recovery prevents whole-connection stalls
  • Faster setup via TLS 1.3 (often 0-RTT)
  • Connection migration when networks change (Wi-Fi ↔ cellular)
  • Reduced head-of-line blocking versus HTTP/2 on TCP
