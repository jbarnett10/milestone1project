HTTP/3 on QUIC

HTTP/3 maps HTTP onto QUIC, a UDP‑based, secure transport that provides per‑stream multiplexing, fast handshakes (1‑RTT/0‑RTT), and connection migration. By moving multiplexing into the transport, HTTP/3 avoids TCP head‑of‑line blocking that can hinder HTTP/2 when packets are lost.

QUIC integrates TLS 1.3 and is widely enabled by major browsers and CDNs, improving performance especially on mobile and lossy networks.

At a Glance

  • Stream‑level recovery prevents whole‑connection stalls
  • Faster setup via TLS 1.3 (often 0‑RTT)
  • Connection migration when networks change (Wi‑Fi ↔ cellular)
