HTTP/3 on QUIC

HTTP/3 maps HTTP onto QUIC, a UDP-based, encrypted transport protocol that provides per-stream multiplexing, fast handshakes (1-RTT/0-RTT), and connection migration. By handling multiplexing at the transport layer, HTTP/3 avoids the head-of-line blocking that limited HTTP/2's performance when packets were lost.

QUIC integrates TLS 1.3 and is widely supported by browsers and CDNs, improving speed and stability on mobile and lossy networks. Stream-level recovery means a lost packet doesn't stall other requests, making HTTP/3 ideal for variable connections.

Together, these innovations make HTTP/3 the fastest and most resilient version of the protocol to date.

At a Glance

  • Stream-level recovery prevents whole-connection stalls
  • Faster setup via TLS 1.3 (often 0-RTT)
  • Connection migration when networks change (Wi-Fi ↔ cellular)
